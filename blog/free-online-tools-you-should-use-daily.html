Free Online Tools You Should Use Every Day in 2026

April 14, 2026 7 min read

Most people are still spending time on repetitive digital tasks that free tools could handle instantly. In 2026, there's no reason to manually count words, search for currency rates, or open bloated software just to resize a photo. Here are the free online tools worth bookmarking and using every day.

1. Word Counter — For Writers and Students

If you write anything — emails, essays, social media posts, or reports — a live word counter is indispensable. Paste your text and instantly see word count, character count, and characters without spaces. Great for hitting Twitter's 280 character limit, LinkedIn posts, or meeting your university assignment word count.

2. Password Generator — For Security-Conscious Users

Using the same password across multiple sites is one of the biggest security risks in 2026. A password generator creates a strong, randomized password in one click. Set the length, choose whether to include uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols, and generate a password that no hacker could guess.

3. Currency Converter — For Shoppers and Travelers

Whether you're shopping from an international store, sending money abroad, or traveling, you need accurate exchange rates. A free currency converter with live rates covers over 160 currencies and updates automatically. Check the rate, make your decision, and move on — all in under 10 seconds.

4. QR Code Generator — For Sharing Links Instantly

Sharing a link verbally or by typing it out is slow and error-prone. A QR code generator lets you create a scannable code from any URL in seconds. Perfect for sharing your portfolio, a product page, a contact form, or any web content with people nearby — no typing required on their end.

5. Unit Converter — For Everyday Calculations

How many kilometers is 5 miles? How many kilograms is 150 pounds? What is 37°C in Fahrenheit? These are questions that come up constantly in daily life, cooking, fitness, and travel. A unit converter that handles length, weight, temperature, speed, and area saves you from mental math mistakes every time.

6. Image Resizer — For Social Media and Email

Different platforms require different image sizes. A profile picture for LinkedIn has different requirements than a banner for Twitter or a thumbnail for YouTube. A browser-based image resizer lets you upload any image, set the exact pixel dimensions, and download the resized version — without installing any software.

7. Case Converter — For Formatting Text Fast

Ever need to capitalize every word in a heading, or convert a block of text to lowercase because it was accidentally typed in ALL CAPS? A case converter handles this in one click. It's one of those tools you don't think about until you need it — and then you use it constantly.

8. BMI Calculator — For Health Tracking

Body Mass Index is a quick, standardized health metric used by doctors, fitness trainers, and researchers worldwide. A free BMI calculator takes your weight and height and instantly tells you your BMI and whether you fall in the underweight, normal, overweight, or obese range. Useful for anyone tracking their fitness journey.

9. Color Picker — For Designers and Developers

Designers and frontend developers constantly need to convert between HEX, RGB, and HSL color formats. A browser-based color picker shows all three values simultaneously and updates in real time as you select colors. No more manual conversion formulas or hunting through design software menus.

10. Typing Speed Tester — For Continuous Improvement

Spending 10 minutes a day on a typing speed test is one of the highest-ROI productivity habits you can build. Faster typing means faster writing, faster coding, and faster communication across everything you do digitally. Track your WPM and accuracy regularly and watch your speed climb over weeks.
