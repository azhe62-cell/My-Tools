What is a QR Code and how does it work?

April 14, 2026 4 min read

In today's fast-paced digital world, you've likely seen them everywhere—from restaurant menus to billboards. But what exactly is a QR code, and what makes it so revolutionary?

1. Understanding the Basics

QR is short for 'Quick Response.' Unlike a traditional barcode that holds data strictly horizontally, a QR code stores data in a two-dimensional matrix. This fundamental difference allows it to store immense amounts of data—such as URLs, raw text, or contact information—while remaining exceptionally fast to scan.

2. How Does Scanning Work?

Your smartphone camera reads the QR code by identifying the three large square 'positioning markers' in its corners. These squares tell the camera exactly how the code is oriented. Once the code's boundaries are laid out, the device quickly decrypts the intricate grid of black and white pixels into a readable format.

3. The Technology Inside

QR codes employ Reed-Solomon error correction. This means that even if a QR code is partially obscured, damaged, or poorly printed, it can still generally be scanned with complete accuracy! Higher correction levels provide redundancy up to 30%.
