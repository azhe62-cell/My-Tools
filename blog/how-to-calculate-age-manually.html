How to calculate age manually

April 14, 2026 4 min read

Need to compute a rapid chronology constraint but don't have an automated calculator on hand? Here's the raw math behind age metrics.

1. The Root Calculation

The standard algorithm subtracts the birth year from the current year. However, this only gives an estimate. If the current month is earlier than the birth month, the person hasn't had their birthday yet, so you must subtract 1 from the total year count.

2. Month and Day Granularity

To achieve exact months and days, you trace forward. Calculate the difference in months. If the days are negative resulting from the calendar differences, borrow a month. You must also account for varying month lengths (28, 30, or 31 days).

3. The Case for Automation

Factoring in leap years and exact day differentials leaves a lot of room for manual error. Utilizing an online Chronology Engine guarantees absolute granular accuracy in a fraction of a second.
