How to Calculate Age Manually: Step-by-Step Guide

April 14, 2026 6 min read

Calculating someone's exact age sounds simple — until you try to account for months and days. Whether you need it for a document, a medical form, or just curiosity, this guide walks you through the exact process step by step.

The Basic Formula

At its simplest, age is calculated by subtracting the year of birth from the current year. But this only gives you a rough number. To get the exact age in years, months, and days you need to account for whether the birthday has passed yet this year.

Simple formula: Current Year − Birth Year = Age (approximate)

For example: if someone was born in 2000 and the current year is 2026, the rough age is 26. But if their birthday falls in October and today is June, they are still 25.

Step-by-Step: Exact Age Calculation

Let's say the date of birth is March 15, 1998 and today's date is June 12, 2026.

Step 1 — Calculate Years:
2026 − 1998 = 28. Since March 15 has already passed in 2026 (before June 12), the person is 28 years old.

Step 2 — Calculate Months:
June (6) − March (3) = 3 months.

Step 3 — Calculate Days:
12 − 15 = −3. Since this is negative, we borrow from the months. May has 31 days, so: 31 − 15 + 12 = 28 days. Months become 3 − 1 = 2.

Result: 28 years, 2 months, 28 days.

What If the Birthday Hasn't Happened Yet This Year?

If the current month and day are before the birth month and day, subtract one extra year from your result. For example, if today is February 1, 2026 and the birthday is March 15, 1998, the person has not yet turned 28 this year — so they are still 27.

When Month Subtraction Goes Negative

If the birth month is greater than the current month, you need to borrow 12 months (one year) from your years total. For example, if someone was born in November and today is February: 2 − 11 = −9. So you add 12: −9 + 12 = 3 months, and subtract 1 from the years.

Common Use Cases for Exact Age Calculation

  • Medical records — Doctors need exact ages for dosage calculations and diagnosis criteria
  • Legal documents — Contracts, wills, and certificates often require precise age
  • Insurance forms — Age determines premium categories
  • Academic eligibility — Scholarships and programs often have age cutoff dates
  • Retirement planning — Exact age determines pension eligibility
