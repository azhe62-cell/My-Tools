Are QR Codes safe?

April 14, 2026 4 min read

With the rise of touchless menus, mobile payments, and digital information passing, QR codes are indispensable. But should you be worried about safety?

1. The Inherent Safety of the Code

The square matrix image itself is inherently completely safe—it is just data visually encoded. It cannot contain an 'executable' virus. The true question of safety lies entirely within the data the QR code holds (such as the URL destination).

2. Common Phishing Attacks

A practice known as 'quishing' (QR Phishing) involves scammers placing malicious QR code stickers over legitimate ones. Scanning them takes the user to a fake website designed to steal login credentials or credit card data.

3. How to Protect Yourself

Always verify the destination domain. When you scan a QR code with your iPhone or Android, the operating system will normally show you a preview of the URL before opening it. Make sure you recognize and trust that URL before you tap it.
