Unit Converter Guide: Length, Weight, Temperature & More

June 12, 2026 7 min read

The world runs on two major measurement systems — metric and imperial — and they rarely agree. Whether you're traveling, cooking, studying science, or just trying to understand a recipe from another country, unit conversion comes up constantly. Here's a practical guide to the most common conversions.

Metric vs Imperial: A Quick Overview

The metric system (used by most of the world) is based on units of 10 — meters, kilometers, grams, kilograms, liters — making conversions within the system straightforward (just move the decimal point). The imperial system (used primarily in the United States) uses units like feet, miles, pounds, and Fahrenheit, with conversion factors that aren't based on round numbers, making mental math harder.

Length Conversions

Length is one of the most frequently converted measurements, especially for travel and international communication:

  • 1 kilometer = 0.6214 miles
  • 1 mile = 1.609 kilometers
  • 1 meter = 3.281 feet
  • 1 foot = 0.3048 meters = 30.48 cm
  • 1 inch = 2.54 centimeters
  • 1 yard = 0.9144 meters

Common use case: Road signs in the US show miles, while most of the world uses kilometers. A speed limit of 60 mph is approximately 97 km/h.

Weight Conversions

  • 1 kilogram = 2.205 pounds
  • 1 pound = 0.4536 kilograms = 453.6 grams
  • 1 ounce = 28.35 grams
  • 1 metric ton = 1000 kilograms = 2204.6 pounds

Common use case: Body weight, shipping weights, and recipe ingredients are common areas where kg and lb conversions matter — a person weighing 70 kg weighs approximately 154 lbs.

Temperature Conversions

Unlike length and weight, temperature scales don't share a common zero point, so conversions require formulas rather than simple multiplication:

Celsius to Fahrenheit: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

Fahrenheit to Celsius: °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9

Celsius to Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15

  • 0°C = 32°F (water freezes)
  • 100°C = 212°F (water boils)
  • 37°C = 98.6°F (normal body temperature)
  • 20°C = 68°F (comfortable room temperature)

Common use case: Weather forecasts, oven temperatures for recipes (a recipe calling for 180°C is roughly 350°F — a very common baking temperature), and medical contexts.

Speed Conversions

  • 1 km/h = 0.6214 mph = 0.2778 m/s
  • 1 mph = 1.609 km/h = 0.4470 m/s
  • 1 knot = 1.852 km/h = 1.151 mph (used in aviation and maritime navigation)

Area Conversions

  • 1 square meter = 10.764 square feet
  • 1 hectare = 10,000 square meters = 2.471 acres
  • 1 acre = 4,046.86 square meters = 0.4047 hectares

Common use case: Real estate listings often use different area units depending on country — square meters, square feet, acres, or hectares — making conversion essential when comparing property sizes internationally.

Why Manual Conversion is Error-Prone

Unlike length and weight conversions (which are simple multiplication), temperature conversions involve both multiplication and addition — easy to get wrong, especially the order of operations. Mixing up the direction of a conversion (multiplying instead of dividing, or vice versa) is also a common error that can lead to wildly incorrect results, particularly problematic in contexts like cooking (wrong oven temperature) or medicine (wrong dosage units).
